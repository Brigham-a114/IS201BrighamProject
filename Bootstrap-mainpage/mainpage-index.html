About Utah

Although it is considered a desert state, in the winter Utah will get upwards of 500 inches of snow each year, making for some epic weekends and fun vacations.

Utah is home to 21 total ski resorts and over 900 kilometers of total slopes to ski. It is no wonder that people travel from all over the world to get a taste of the thrill.

Brighton Resort

Open since 1936, Brighton is home to 1050 acres packed to the brim with 66 trails and 8 lifts.

After 4pm, Brighton also hosts night skiing, lining the trails with 250 lights so skiers and snowboarders can shred snow to their hearts content.

Snowbird

Tucked away in Little Cottonwood canyon, Snowbird is one of the bigger resorts with a total of 2500 acres of skiable terrain. Before it's opening in 1971, it was one of the largest silver mines in the Wasatch Mountains. Full of history, the resort boasts a magic carpet tunnel that runs through the mountain, an aerial tram, 14 lifts, and 169 trails.

Solitude

In the early 1950's, Robert M. Barrett made a fortune mining uranium in Big Cottonwood canyon. His dream to establish his own ski resort came true and Solitude Mountain Resort was established in 1957. Neighboring Brighton, Solitude hosts 8 lifts and 82 trails.

Alta

For all the Skiers out there! Alta is one of the three ski-only resorts in the United States since 1938. In 2001, Alta partnered with Snowbird to allow skiers to buy one ticket for both resorts. This meants that skiers have access to 4700 acres of terrain and 26 chairlifts.
